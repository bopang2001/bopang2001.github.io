**2025 - 2026**  
**Zhejiang University**

This research experience studies place recognition when the available map is sparse, incomplete, or compact enough for deployment-oriented robot navigation. The goal is to support robust LiDAR localization and loop closure detection without relying on dense, fully reconstructed maps.

- Investigated LiDAR-based localization under sparse map conditions, treating sparse maps as compact and deployment-oriented representations for map-light navigation.
- Designed bird's-eye-view representations that combine geometric occupancy and semantic cues for reliable retrieval under incomplete map observations.
- Explored invariance-aware global descriptors for large-displacement loop closure detection under sparse, partial, and cross-view observations.

## Semantic-aware Sparse-map Retrieval for LiDAR Place Recognition

**Status:** manuscript in revision for journal resubmission.

The work builds semantic-enhanced spatial representations for sparse-map retrieval. It studies how semantic cues and BEV-style spatial structure can be combined into a compact global descriptor for robust retrieval under partial observations, map sparsity, and cross-view changes.
