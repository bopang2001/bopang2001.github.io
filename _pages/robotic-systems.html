Mobile Robot Platform Debugging

Hands-on experience with UGV and intelligent vehicle platform setup and debugging, including LiDAR setup, chassis control, onboard computing, power/signal interconnects, and data acquisition.

Work includes bringing up and debugging mobile robot, UGV, and intelligent vehicle platforms by connecting sensors, onboard computers, chassis control, power systems, and data-acquisition workflows for real-platform perception experiments.

  • Jackal-based UGV platform: connected an onboard computer with a Velodyne VLP-16 LiDAR sensor and chassis-control interface. Also integrated a laptop-class compute platform with an Intel 14600H-class CPU and an RTX 4060 GPU onto the same robot, including power delivery, signal connections, and system-level interaction.
  • Ranger Mini-class UGV platform: worked with a Livox LiDAR setup and communication with an Intel N100-class onboard computing unit for mobile robot perception and data acquisition.
  • ARM-based compute boards: experience with Raspberry Pi 4B, RK3566, and RK3588 computing boards for embedded robotic systems and platform-side computing.
  • MCU-based racing vehicle: developed and debugged video-signal processing and low-level control for an MCU-based racing vehicle platform.

PCB & Embedded Power Electronics

Compact power and interface modules for robotic systems, including high-current DC-DC conversion, onboard compute power delivery, low-noise supplies, and battery-powered debugging utilities.

Experience designing compact PCB-based power and interface modules for mobile robotic systems, especially where high-current power delivery, limited space, and reliable field debugging are important.

  • High-current DC-DC buck module for mobile compute payloads: designed a compact DC-DC buck module rated at 600 W with over 800 W peak output, providing 19 V / 30 A+ and 12 V / 8 A outputs. The module reaches over 98% measured efficiency excluding cable loss, with an 80 mm x 49 mm footprint. It is intended for powering laptop-class compute platforms on mobile robots.
  • ATX 3.1 DC-DC power module for desktop-class onboard computing: designed an ATX 3.1-compatible DC-DC buck module rated at 800 W with over 1000 W peak output, intended for mobile robotic platforms carrying a full desktop PC system.
  • Miniature low-noise power module: designed a 3.3 W low-noise power module with an 8.3 mm x 6.4 mm footprint and output ripple below 10 mV Vpp, targeting space-constrained robotic systems that require clean local power.
  • Wide-input Li-ion battery charge-discharge module: designed a 2.5-7 V wide-input lithium-ion battery charge-discharge module with an integrated cell, suitable for compact uninterruptible power supply applications.
  • Battery-powered USB debugging hub: designed a 12-port USB power hub with up to 60 W output, intended for battery-powered field debugging and long-duration outdoor testing.
